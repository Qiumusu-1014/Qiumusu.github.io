Strategic $500M Investment in Green Hydrogen Infrastructure

DUBAI, UAE — UAE Royal Capital Funding has officially announced a landmark partnership to invest $500 million in developing sustainable green hydrogen infrastructure across the GCC region. This strategic move aligns with the UAE's "Net Zero by 2050" initiative.

The funding will support the construction of two major electrolysis plants and a dedicated export terminal. "This is not just an investment in energy; it is an investment in the future of our planet," stated Sheikh Mohammed bin Hamdan Al Nahyan during the press conference.

The project, which is set to break ground in early 2025, is expected to create over 2,000 specialized jobs in engineering and renewable energy sectors. It will also solidify the UAE's position as a global leader in renewable energy export, reducing reliance on traditional fossil fuels while maintaining economic growth.

Furthermore, the Foundation is exploring partnerships with European and Asian markets to establish a secure supply chain for green ammonia, ensuring that the benefits of this investment reach a global scale.
