Palm Jebel Ali: Major Infrastructure Contracts Awarded for "New Dubai"

DUBAI, UAE — Nakheel, a world-leading master developer, has awarded major infrastructure contracts totaling AED 5 billion ($1.36 billion) for the Palm Jebel Ali project, marking a significant acceleration in Dubai's "2040 Urban Master Plan."

The revived mega-project, twice the size of the original Palm Jumeirah, will add 110 kilometers of coastline to Dubai. UAE Royal Capital Funding is participating as a key financing partner for the luxury hospitality district on the crescent.

"Palm Jebel Ali is not just real estate; it is a new destination that redefines luxury living," said the Foundation's Head of Real Estate. "Our investment ensures that sustainable building practices are integrated into the core infrastructure."

Construction on the access bridges and utility networks is set to begin next month, with the first wave of luxury villas expected to be handed over by 2027.
